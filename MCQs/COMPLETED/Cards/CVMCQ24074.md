### ü´Ä Cardiology: Constrictive Pericarditis Treatment  

#### ‚úÖ True Statements  
1. **Chronic constrictive pericarditis** is treated definitively with surgical **pericardial stripping (pericardiectomy)**.  
2. **Transient constrictive pericarditis** presents with pericardial constriction plus systemic or pericardial inflammation (for example, fever or elevated inflammatory markers) and is managed like **acute pericarditis** using **anti-inflammatory therapy** and **diuretics**.  
3. Chronic constrictive pericarditis is characterized by a **fibrotic or calcified pericardium** that restricts diastolic filling and limits total cardiac volume.  
4. Constrictive pericarditis most commonly manifests with **right-sided heart failure** and **systemic volume overload**, including ascites.  
5. **Pericardial thickening, fibrosis, or calcification** produce ventricular interdependence and impair diastolic ventricular filling in constrictive pericarditis.  

#### üí¨ Extra  
1. Ventricular interdependence can be demonstrated by Doppler echocardiography, cardiac magnetic resonance imaging, or invasive hemodynamic evaluation in constrictive pericarditis but is absent in restrictive cardiomyopathy.  
2. Anti-inflammatory drugs such as **ibuprofen** and **colchicine** are reserved for acute or transient pericardial inflammation and are ineffective in chronic fibrotic constriction.
