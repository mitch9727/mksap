#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Pulmonary valve stenosis** causes obstruction of the **right ventricular (RV) outflow tract** and is usually an **isolated valve lesion**.
2. **Mild pulmonary valve stenosis** is typically **asymptomatic**, well tolerated, and rarely progresses to severe stenosis.
3. **Moderate to severe pulmonary valve stenosis** can cause **dyspnea** and **exercise intolerance**.
4. A **systolic ejection click** that **decreases during inspiration** is characteristic of **pulmonary valve stenosis**.
5. A **crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur** at the **upper left sternal border** is characteristic of **pulmonary valve stenosis**.
6. The **systolic ejection click** results from **restricted opening** of **tethered pulmonary valve leaflets**.
7. During **inspiration**, increased venous return reduces leaflet excursion, softening the **systolic ejection click**.
8. The **intensity of the murmur** generally correlates with the **severity of pulmonary valve stenosis**.
9. As severity increases, the **pulmonary component of S2** is **delayed** and may **eventually disappear**.
10. A **parasternal lift** suggestive of **RV hypertrophy** may be present in significant **pulmonary valve stenosis**.
11. **Electrocardiogram (ECG)** findings in **pulmonary valve stenosis** may reflect **RV hypertrophy** in the presence of significant obstruction.
