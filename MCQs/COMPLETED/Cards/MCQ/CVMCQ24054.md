
### ü´† Peripheral Artery Disease: Chronic Limb-Threatening Ischemia and Anatomic Testing

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Chronic limb-threatening ischemia (CLTI)** warrants **anatomic testing**, such as **computed tomography angiography (CTA) of the abdomen with runoff**, to determine the feasibility and approach for **revascularization**.
2. **CTA of the abdomen with runoff** provides detailed anatomic information to guide **endovascular or surgical revascularization** for **peripheral artery disease (PAD)**.
3. Patients with **CLTI**, including those with **rest pain**, **tissue ulceration**, or **gangrene**, should be referred for **revascularization** if symptoms persist despite standard therapy.
4. **Below-the-knee amputation** should not be the initial step in patients with **CLTI** who are candidates for **revascularization**.
5. **Toe-brachial index testing** is reserved for patients with **noncompressible vessels** on ankle-brachial index (ABI) testing or to assess **wound healing potential**.
6. **Intravenous heparin** is not indicated in **chronic ischemia** without signs of **acute limb ischemia**.
