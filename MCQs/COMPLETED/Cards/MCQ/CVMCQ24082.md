2. **Tuberculous pericarditis** should be considered in patients with **risk factors**, including **prior residence in a high-prevalence area**.
3. **Pericardiocentesis** should be performed in suspected **tuberculous pericardial effusion** to establish the diagnosis.
4. Pericardial fluid from pericardiocentesis should be sent for **acid-fast bacilli (AFB) stain and culture**, **nucleic acid amplification tests**, and **adenosine deaminase levels**.
5. **Pericardial drainage** is indicated in suspected tuberculous pericarditis due to the risk of **cardiac tamponade** and **constrictive pericarditis**.
6. **Pericardiocentesis** is the initial step in managing **large, presumed inflammatory pericardial effusions**.
7. **Open pericardiectomy** or creation of a **pericardial window** is considered if pericardiocentesis is **unsuccessful**, **unsafe**, or if the effusion is **recurrent**.
8. **Right heart catheterization** is **not indicated** for evaluating pericardial effusion; **transthoracic echocardiography (TTE)** is typically adequate.
9. **Transesophageal echocardiography (TEE)** is generally **not required** unless **TTE is inadequate**.
