### ü´Ä Cardiovascular Complications: Cardiac Tamponade Post-Ablation

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Cardiac tamponade** is the most common serious complication and most common cause of death following **catheter ablation for atrial fibrillation**, occurring in approximately **1% of patients**.
2. **Cardiac tamponade** can occur **within hours to weeks** following catheter ablation, with a mean onset of **10 days**.
3. Clinical features of **cardiac tamponade** include **hypotension**, **elevated central venous pressure**, **narrow pulse pressure**, **pulsus paradoxus**, and **distant heart sounds**.
4. **Echocardiography** is the **primary diagnostic modality** for **cardiac tamponade** and readily detects **pericardial effusions**.
5. **ECG findings** in cardiac tamponade include **sinus tachycardia** and **electrical alternans**.
6. **Chest radiograph** may show an **enlarged cardiac silhouette** in cardiac tamponade.
7. **Urgent pericardial drainage** is the treatment of choice for **cardiac tamponade**, performed by **pericardiocentesis** or **surgical subxiphoid approach**.
8. **Pericardiocentesis** is typically guided by **echocardiography** or **fluoroscopy**.
9. **Surgical therapy** is indicated when pericardiocentesis cannot be performed safely, or for **diagnostic tissue sampling** or **prevention of recurrent effusions**.
10. In **hemodynamically unstable patients**, **intravenous normal saline** can be used temporarily to stabilize circulation before definitive therapy.
11. **Pulsus paradoxus**, defined as a **systolic blood pressure drop >10 mm Hg during inspiration**, is a key clinical sign of **cardiac tamponade**.
12. **Early diastolic right ventricular collapse**, **late diastolic right atrial collapse**, and **abnormal interventricular septal motion** are echocardiographic features associated with **cardiac tamponade**.
13. **Doppler echocardiography** may show a **>25% drop in mitral inflow velocity during inspiration**, the echocardiographic equivalent of **pulsus paradoxus**.
14. In **rapid-onset tamponade** (e.g., trauma, dissection, invasive procedures), small pericardial fluid volumes can cause significant hemodynamic compromise.
15. **Right heart catheterization** may show **equalized diastolic pressures** and **blunting of the y descent** on the right atrial waveform in tamponade.
