### ü´† Vascular Disease: Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Surveillance

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Abdominal aortic aneurysms (AAAs)** with a diameter smaller than **4.0 cm** have an estimated **5-year rupture risk of 2%**.
2. **Repeat abdominal aortic ultrasonography** every **2 to 3 years** is appropriate surveillance for **asymptomatic AAAs <4.0 cm** in diameter.
3. The **Society for Vascular Surgery** recommends a **3-year surveillance interval** for AAAs measuring **3.0 to 4.0 cm** in both men and women.
4. For **AAA diameters of 4.0 to 4.9 cm in men**, or **4.0 to 4.4 cm in women**, **surveillance imaging is recommended annually**.
5. For **AAA diameters of 5.0 to <5.5 cm in men**, or **4.5 to <5.0 cm in women**, **surveillance imaging should occur every 6 months**.
6. **Surgical or endovascular repair** is indicated when **AAA diameter reaches 5.5 cm in men** or **5.0 cm in women** due to elevated rupture risk.
7. **AAA repair is also indicated** in patients with **symptoms** (e.g., abdominal tenderness or pain) or **rapid aneurysm expansion** (>0.5 cm/year).
8. **Computed tomography (CT) angiography** is used to define the **anatomic location** of the AAA (suprarenal, juxtarenal, or infrarenal) during **preoperative planning**, not routine surveillance.
9. **Discontinuing surveillance** of an asymptomatic patient with AAA is inappropriate due to the ongoing **risk of rupture and death**.
10. **Surveillance ultrasonography** is recommended for **men with AAA <5.5 cm** and **women with AAA <5.0 cm**, with imaging interval determined by **aneurysm size**.

1. The low rupture risk of small aneurysms allows for safe, infrequent monitoring.
2. There is no single optimal surveillance interval defined by trials, but consensus recommendations guide timing.
3. Society recommendations are based on expert opinion in the absence of large randomized trials.
4. Annual surveillance balances detection of aneurysm growth with avoidance of excessive testing.
5. Shorter intervals for larger aneurysms detect rapid changes that may require surgical intervention.
6. Aneurysms ‚â•5.5 cm (men) or ‚â•5.0 cm (women) present significant risk of rupture, justifying repair.
7. Symptom presence or rapid growth increases rupture risk even if below standard surgical thresholds.

9. Ongoing surveillance prevents missed opportunities for timely intervention as aneurysms grow.
10. The surveillance interval is tiered based on maximum aneurysm diameter, stratified by sex.
