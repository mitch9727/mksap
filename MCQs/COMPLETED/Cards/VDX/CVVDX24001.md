1. **Multifocal atrial tachycardia (MAT)** is most commonly seen in **acutely ill patients**, especially in the setting of **pulmonary disease** such as **chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)** exacerbation.
2. **Electrocardiogram (ECG)** findings in MAT include **at least three different P-wave morphologies**, **varying P‚ÄìP, P‚ÄìR, and R‚ÄìR intervals**, and a **heart rate >100/min**.
3. **MAT** is distinguished from **atrial fibrillation** by the presence of **discernible P waves** and a **1:1 P wave to QRS complex relationship**.
4. **MAT** is distinguished from **wandering atrial pacemaker** by a **heart rate greater than 100/min**.
5. The morphologic features of **P waves in MAT** are best seen in **leads II, III, and V1** on ECG.
6. **Treatment of MAT** focuses on correcting the **underlying disease**, as the arrhythmia is often **refractory or recurrent** without it.

1. Associated factors include **hypoxia** and **electrolyte abnormalities**.
2. These ECG characteristics help differentiate MAT from **junctional tachycardia**, which lacks P-wave morphology variation.
3. Unlike in atrial fibrillation, **MAT has organized atrial activity**.
4. **Wandering atrial pacemaker** is defined by similar P-wave variability but occurs at a **slower heart rate**.
6. Common contributors to recurrence include **untreated pulmonary pathology** and **metabolic disturbances**.
