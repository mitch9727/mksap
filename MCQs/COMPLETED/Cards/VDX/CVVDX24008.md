### ü´† Congenital Heart Disease: Ostium Secundum Atrial Septal Defect (ASD) and ECG Findings

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Ostium secundum atrial septal defect (ASD)** is the **most common type of ASD**, accounting for approximately **75% of cases**, and is located in the **mid portion of the atrial septum**.
2. **Symptoms** of ostium secundum atrial septal defect (ASD) may include **fatigue**, **exertional dyspnea**, **arrhythmias**, and **paradoxical embolism**.
3. Clinical examination findings in patients with ostium secundum atrial septal defect (ASD) may include a **parasternal impulse**, **fixed splitting of the second heart sound (S2)**, and a **pulmonary outflow murmur**.
4. A **diastolic flow rumble across the tricuspid valve** may occur in patients with ostium secundum atrial septal defect (ASD) when there is a **large left-to-right shunt**.
5. Typical **electrocardiogram (ECG)** findings in ostium secundum atrial septal defect (ASD) include **right axis deviation** (e.g., **negative QRS in lead I** and **positive QRS in leads II, III, and aVF**) and **incomplete right bundle branch block** (e.g., **QRS duration <120 ms**, **prominent S wave in lateral leads**, and **prominent qR complex in lead V1**).

#### üí¨ Extra
1. Ostium secundum ASDs are typically **isolated congenital anomalies**, unlike primum or sinus venosus defects that are often associated with other anomalies.
4. A left-to-right shunt increases right-sided blood flow, leading to augmented **diastolic flow across the tricuspid valve**.
5. Right axis deviation and incomplete right bundle branch block are consequences of **right ventricular volume overload** due to the ASD.
