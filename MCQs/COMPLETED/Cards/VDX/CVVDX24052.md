1. The **third heart sound (S3)** is a **low-frequency**, **early diastolic** sound best heard with the **bell of the stethoscope** at the **cardiac apex**.
2. An **S3** may be perceived as a **double S2** and is best heard when the patient is in the **left lateral decubitus position**.
3. The **S3 heart sound** is often heard in **heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF)**.
4. The **S3 sound** may also be present in **volume overload states**, such as **valvular regurgitation**, **shunt lesions**, or **late pregnancy**.
5. An **S3** can be a **normal finding** in **children** or **highly trained athletes**.
6. A **split second heart sound (S2)** consists of **high-frequency** components best heard with the **diaphragm** in the **second left intercostal space**.
7. **Physiologic splitting of S2** varies with the **respiratory cycle**, being heard as a **single S2** during expiration and a **double S2** during inspiration.
8. **Paradoxical splitting of S2** occurs when the split is heard during **expiration** and a **single S2** during **inspiration**, commonly seen in **aortic stenosis**, **left bundle branch block**, and **right ventricular pacing**.
9. The **fourth heart sound (S4)** is a **low-frequency** sound that occurs in **late diastole** and may be perceived as a **double S1**.
