### ü´† Cardiac Rhythm Disorders: Premature Atrial Contractions (PACs)

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Premature atrial contractions (PACs)** are early isolated beats that originate from the atria.
2. **Premature atrial contractions (PACs)** are exceedingly common and increase in frequency with age.
3. **Premature atrial contractions (PACs)** can produce an **irregular pulse** by being either **conducted**, leading to an early pulse, or **blocked** in the atrioventricular node, resulting in **compensatory pauses**.
4. The presence of **discrete P waves** on electrocardiogram (ECG) excludes the diagnosis of **atrial fibrillation**.
5. The absence of **premature wide QRS complexes** on ECG excludes the diagnosis of **premature ventricular contractions (PVCs)**.
6. The presence of **premature P waves with morphology differing from sinus rhythm** excludes **sinus arrhythmia** as the cause of irregular pulse.

#### üí¨ Extra
1. PACs are often benign but can trigger more significant arrhythmias in predisposed individuals.
3. Conducted PACs may produce palpitations, while blocked PACs can lead to pauses perceived as skipped beats.
4. Atrial fibrillation is characterized by absent P waves and an irregularly irregular rhythm.
5. PVCs typically appear as early, wide QRS complexes with discordant T waves.
6. Sinus arrhythmia involves gradual changes in P-P interval due to respiratory variation, not discrete ectopic P waves.
