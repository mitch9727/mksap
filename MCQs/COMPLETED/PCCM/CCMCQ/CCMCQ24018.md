### ü´Å Critical Care Medicine: Noninvasive Ventilation in Cardiogenic Pulmonary Edema

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema** usually responds promptly to therapy with **diuretics** and **vasodilators**.  
2. **Noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation** (continuous positive airway pressure [CPAP] or bilevel positive airway pressure) **hastens the resolution of hypoxemia**, **reduces preload and afterload**, **improves symptoms**, **lowers intubation rates**, and **decreases mortality** compared with **oxygen therapy alone**.  
3. Noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation should be considered in **all patients with acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema and hypoxemic respiratory failure** who do not respond promptly to appropriate medical therapy.  
4. **Loop diuretics** may be continued in acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema, but there is **no evidence** for more rapid symptom control when administered as **continuous infusion versus bolus dosing**.  
5. **Endotracheal intubation and mechanical ventilation** may be necessary if a patient does not respond to medical therapy and CPAP or is intolerant of CPAP.  
6. In **acute decompensated heart failure**, **pharmacologic therapy** is superior to **ultrafiltration** for control of symptoms and preservation of kidney function, and ultrafiltration is associated with a higher complication rate.  
7. In cardiogenic pulmonary edema, **noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation** reduces the need for intubation and **lowers mortality** compared with oxygen therapy alone.

1. **Acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema** often presents with **hypoxemic respiratory failure**.  
2. All patients presenting with **acute respiratory failure** should be evaluated for **cardiogenic causes**, including **cardiomyopathy**, **mitral valve disease**, **aortic valve disease**, **myocardial ischemia**, and **arrhythmias** (especially atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular rate).  
3. Evaluation of acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema should include: assessment for **volume overload** (jugular venous distention, S3, peripheral edema), **electrocardiogram (ECG)**, **B-type natriuretic peptide (BNP)** level, **serial serum troponin levels**, and **echocardiography**.  
4. In acute cardiogenic pulmonary edema, therapy to **reduce preload and afterload** with **vasodilators** or **inodilators** produces rapid improvement, while **diuretics** play a role in longer-term management of fluid overload.  
5. Systematic reviews show that **noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation** in cardiogenic pulmonary edema **hastens resolution of hypoxemia**, **reduces preload and afterload**, **improves symptoms**, **lowers intubation rates**, and **decreases mortality** compared with **oxygen therapy alone**.
