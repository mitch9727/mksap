1. **Postoperative atelectasis** is a common cause of **hypoxemia** after **upper abdominal surgery**.  
2. Risk factors for **postoperative atelectasis** include **smoking**, **obesity**, and **older age**.  
3. **Shallow breathing**, **weak cough** due to pain, **diaphragmatic dysfunction**, and **anesthesia-induced impairment of the mucociliary escalator** predispose to **respiratory secretion accumulation** and **reduced functional residual capacity (FRC)**.  
4. **Atelectasis** occurs when **functional residual capacity** drops below **closing volume** during tidal breaths.  
5. Management of **postoperative atelectasis** includes **pain control**, **early mobility**, **secretion management**, and **respiratory physiotherapy**.  
6. **Respiratory physiotherapy** methods for atelectasis prevention and treatment include **inspiratory muscle training**, **deep breathing exercise**, **mobility programs**, and **percussion and vibration therapy**.  
7. For patients with **postoperative atelectasis**, **bronchoscopy** for routine airway mucous clearance offers **no clear benefit** compared with chest physiotherapy.  
8. **Bronchoscopy** should be considered only if **24 hours of chest physiotherapy** fails to improve atelectasis.
