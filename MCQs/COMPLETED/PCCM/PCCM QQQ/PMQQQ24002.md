### ü´Å Pulmonary Medicine: Diagnosis of Bronchiectasis

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Bronchiectasis** should be considered in the differential diagnosis of any patient with a **chronic cough**, especially if there is a history of **frequent respiratory infections** or if the cough is **productive**.  
2. Additional symptoms of **bronchiectasis** include **hemoptysis**, **wheezing**, and **dyspnea**.  
3. Physical examination in **bronchiectasis** may reveal **clubbing**, **crackles**, and/or **wheezing**.  
4. **Pulmonary function tests (PFTs)** in **bronchiectasis** often demonstrate **mild to moderate fixed airflow obstruction**.  
5. **Chest radiography** in **bronchiectasis** may show **linear atelectasis** or **dilated and thickened airways** (seen as **‚Äútram‚Äù or ‚Äúparallel‚Äù lines**).  
6. **High-resolution computed tomography (HRCT) of the chest** is the **gold standard diagnostic test** for **bronchiectasis**.  
7. Diagnostic criteria for **bronchiectasis on HRCT** include an **airway diameter greater than the accompanying vessel** and **lack of distal airway tapering**.  
8. **Bronchial wall thickening**, **mucus impaction**, and **cysts** may also be present on **HRCT** in **bronchiectasis**.  
9. Previous **sputum cultures** with uncommon pathogens such as **Pseudomonas aeruginosa**, **Aspergillus**, or **nontuberculous mycobacteria** may be a clue to **bronchiectasis**.  
10. Evaluation for **underlying causes** of **bronchiectasis** should always be performed, though up to **40% of cases are idiopathic**.  

#### üí¨ Extra
1. Bronchoscopy is useful when there is concern for **mass, focal obstruction, or foreign body**, not for routine diagnosis.  
2. **Bronchoalveolar lavage** is considered only if **sputum cultures are negative** but an **infectious etiology** is still suspected.  
3. **Chest MRI** has a very limited role in **bronchiectasis diagnosis** and is generally **not indicated**.  
4. **Sweat chloride testing** is the **initial test for cystic fibrosis (CF)** and should be considered only if **bronchiectasis is confirmed**, especially with **upper lobe distribution**.
