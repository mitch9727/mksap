1. **Cough-variant asthma** refers to **asthma in which the predominant manifestation is cough**, without other typical asthma symptoms such as **wheezing, breathlessness, or chest tightness**.
2. In patients with **chronic cough**, a **methacholine challenge test** is indicated when **spirometry is normal** to evaluate for **bronchial hyperreactivity**, which supports a diagnosis of asthma.
3. A **methacholine challenge test** is considered **positive** if methacholine causes **forced expiratory volume in 1 second (FEV1)** to drop by **20% or more** compared with baseline FEV1.
4. A **positive methacholine challenge test** is not specific enough to diagnose asthma; patients with cough and a positive test must also **respond clinically to asthma therapies** to be considered to have **cough-variant asthma**.
5. **High-resolution chest CT** is used to evaluate for **interstitial lung disease or bronchiectasis** in chronic cough when chest radiograph or examination suggests these conditions, but **asthma should be excluded first** if these features are absent.
6. **CT of the sinuses** is not indicated in chronic cough when **no nasal or sinus symptoms** are reported.
7. **Esophageal manometry and 24-hour pH testing** are not appropriate for chronic cough without **gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) symptoms**; guidelines recommend an **empiric trial of proton pump inhibitors and lifestyle modification** before invasive testing.
8. Common causes of **chronic cough** (in the absence of angiotensin-converting enzyme [ACE] inhibitor use or environmental exposures) include **asthma, GERD, and rhinosinusitis**.
