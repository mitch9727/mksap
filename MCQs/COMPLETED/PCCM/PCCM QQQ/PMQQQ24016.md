1. A **complicated parapneumonic effusion** is an effusion associated with **pneumonia** that has **pleural fluid pH <7.2**, **pleural fluid glucose <60 mg/dL (3.3 mmol/L)**, or **bacterial organisms** confirmed by **positive culture or Gram stain**.
2. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions** occur when **bacteria invade the pleural space**, but Gram stain is typically **negative** and cultures are usually **sterile**.
3. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions** are often **loculated** and **large**.
4. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions** do not resolve with **antibiotics alone** and require **drainage**.
5. Options for drainage of a **complicated parapneumonic effusion** include **thoracostomy tube drainage** and **video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS)**, generally combined with **intrapleural fibrinolytics (tissue plasminogen activator)** and a **mucolytic agent (deoxyribonuclease)**.
6. An **empyema** is a **bacterial infection of the pleural space** that results in **purulent fluid** or a **positive Gram stain**.
7. A **positive pleural fluid culture** is not required for the diagnosis of **empyema** because cultures are **less sensitive** than Gram stain.
8. **Empyema management** involves **drainage**, with options including **thoracostomy tube drainage** and **video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery**, generally with the combined use of **intrapleural fibrinolytics** and a **mucolytic agent**.
9. **Pleural fluid acidosis (pH <7.3)** can occur in **complicated parapneumonic effusions**, **rheumatoid and lupus pleuritis**, **esophageal rupture**, and **malignancy**.
10. A **malignant effusion** can have a **low pleural fluid pH** and **glucose level**.
11. An **uncomplicated parapneumonic effusion** is characterized by **pleural fluid pH >7.2** and **pleural fluid glucose >60 mg/dL (3.3 mmol/L)**.
12. **Uncomplicated parapneumonic effusions** are usually **small** and **free flowing**.
13. **Uncomplicated parapneumonic effusions** do not require **drainage** and typically **resolve with antibiotic therapy alone**.
