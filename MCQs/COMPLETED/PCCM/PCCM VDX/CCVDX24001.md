1. **Atelectasis** is a common **postoperative complication**.
2. Pain or impaired respiratory mechanics after thoracic and abdominal surgery can lead to **shallow breathing** and **cough suppression**, promoting atelectasis.
3. Patients with **atelectasis** may be **asymptomatic** or may present with **labored breathing**, **diminished breath sounds at the lung bases**, **egophony**, **rhonchi**, and **consolidation on imaging**.
4. Management of **atelectasis** includes **pain control**, **early mobility**, and **secretion management**.
5. **Respiratory physiotherapy** (inspiratory muscle training, deep breathing, mobility programs, percussion, vibration therapy) helps **prevent and treat atelectasis**.
6. **Routine bronchoscopy** for airway mucous clearance offers **no clear benefit** compared with other chest physiotherapy and should be reserved for cases not improving after 24 hours of physiotherapy.
7. If secretions are minimal, **positive expiratory pressure devices** may be used to recruit collapsed alveoli in atelectasis.
8. **Mucolytics such as N-acetylcysteine** have **no proven benefit** for atelectasis treatment.
