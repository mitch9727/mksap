#### ‚úÖ True Statements ‚Äî *Common Criteria for Spontaneous Breathing Trials and Extubation*
1. **Spontaneous breathing trials (SBTs)** should only be performed when the **cause of respiratory failure has improved**.  
2. Criteria to perform an **SBT** include **FiO‚ÇÇ ‚â§50%** and **PEEP ‚â§5‚Äì8 cm H‚ÇÇO**.  
3. An **arterial pH >7.25** is required before conducting an SBT.  
4. **Hemodynamic stability** is required before attempting an SBT.  
5. Patients must demonstrate the **ability to breathe spontaneously** before attempting an SBT.  
6. Criteria to pass an **SBT** require at least **30 minutes without clinical evidence of respiratory distress**, including **oxygen saturation <90%** or **respiratory rate >35/min**.  
7. Failure criteria for an **SBT** include **new arrhythmias**, **tachycardia**, or **blood pressure instability** (hypotension or hypertension).  
8. Before extubation, clinicians should assess **quantity of secretions**, **adequacy of cough**, and **mental status**.  

#### üí¨ Extra
2. **FiO‚ÇÇ** = fraction of inspired oxygen; **PEEP** = positive end-expiratory pressure.  
6. Tachypnea and hypoxemia are among the most common signs of SBT failure.  
8. Poor cough or frequent suctioning needs may predict extubation failure.

#### ‚úÖ True Statements ‚Äî *Most Frequently Used Ventilator Modes*
1. **Volume control assist/control (VC-CMVs)** delivers a **set tidal volume** regardless of patient effort, lung compliance, or resistance.  
2. In **VC-CMVs**, all breaths are **mandatory**, and the ventilator always cycles the breath once the set tidal volume is delivered.  
3. **VC-CMVs** ensure **minimum minute ventilation** but may provide less patient comfort because flow is fixed.  
4. **Pressure control assist/control (PC-CMVs)** delivers a **set inspiratory pressure**, with tidal volume depending on **patient effort, lung compliance, and resistance**.  
5. In **PC-CMVs**, all breaths are **mandatory**, and the ventilator cycles the breath after the **preset inspiratory time**.  
6. **PC-CMVs** ensure pressure does not rise above the set limit, serving the goal of **safety**.  
7. **Pressure support with continuous positive airway pressure (PC-CSVs)** allows all breaths to be **spontaneous**, triggered and cycled by the patient.  
8. In **PC-CSVs**, the patient determines the **size and timing of breaths**, promoting **comfort and synchrony**.  
9. **PC-CSVs** can be used to assess **extubation readiness** during spontaneous breathing trials.  
10. **Synchronized intermittent mandatory ventilation (SIMV)** provides a **minimum set number of mandatory breaths** while allowing spontaneous breaths between them.  
11. In **SIMV**, mandatory breaths may be **volume controlled or pressure controlled**, but not both.  
12. **SIMV** supports patient safety by ensuring a **minimum minute ventilation** while permitting spontaneous breathing.  

#### üí¨ Extra
1. In VC-CMVs, inspiratory pressure varies depending on lung compliance and resistance.  
4. PC-CMVs are safer for limiting pressure but may deliver variable tidal volumes.  
7. PC-CSVs are widely used for patient comfort and weaning assessments.  
10. SIMV generally pairs with **pressure support** on spontaneous breaths.
