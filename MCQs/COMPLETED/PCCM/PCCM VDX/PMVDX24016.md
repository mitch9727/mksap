1. A **parapneumonic effusion** is a pleural effusion associated with **bacterial pneumonia**.
2. **Uncomplicated parapneumonic effusions** are typically **small, sterile, and free-flowing**, and they resolve with treatment of pneumonia.
3. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions** occur with **significant pleural inflammation** or when **bacteria invade the pleural space**.
4. An **empyema** is defined as a **bacterial infection of the pleural space**, which results in **purulent pleural fluid** or a **positive Gram stain**.
5. On **computed tomography (CT) imaging**, empyema is characterized by **pleural enhancement around the effusion** creating a **lenticular-shaped opacity**.
6. A **lung abscess** is an **intraparenchymal process**, often containing an **air‚Äìfluid level**, and it has **thickened, irregular walls**, in contrast to empyema.

1. A **parapneumonic effusion** can be **uncomplicated** (sterile and free-flowing) or **complicated** (infected or loculated).
2. **Uncomplicated parapneumonic effusions** are typically **small**, resolve with treatment of pneumonia, and are associated with **pleural fluid pH >7.2 (often >7.4)**, **glucose >60 mg/dL (3.3 mmol/L)**, and **lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) <900 U/L**.
3. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions** show **low pleural fluid pH**, **low glucose**, and **high LDH**.
4. **Pleural fluid cultures** identify pathogens in only **56% of empyema cases**.
5. **Complicated parapneumonic effusions and empyema** usually require **drainage**.
6. **Drainage is required** if pleural fluid contains **frank pus**, a **positive Gram stain or culture**, or has a **pH <7.2**.
7. **Drainage may be considered** for effusions with **pH 7.2‚Äì7.4** if **LDH >900 U/L**.
8. **Drainage options** for complicated parapneumonic effusions and empyema include **thoracostomy tube drainage** and **video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS)**, generally combined with **intrapleural fibrinolytics (tissue plasminogen activator)** and a **mucolytic agent (deoxyribonuclease)**.
9. **Early intervention** improves outcomes in complicated parapneumonic effusions and empyema.
10. A **multidisciplinary team approach** helps guide treatment of complicated parapneumonic effusions and empyema.
11. In **community-acquired pleural infections**, common pathogens include **Streptococcus pneumoniae, Streptococcus pyogenes, Staphylococcus aureus**, and the **Streptococcus anginosus group**.
12. In **hospital-acquired empyema**, **methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA)** and **Enterobacteriaceae** are more prevalent.
13. **Anaerobic bacteria** are cultured in more than **20% of pleural space infections**.
14. **Polymicrobial infections** are common in pleural space infection.
15. **Empiric antibiotic regimens** for pleural infection should include **coverage for anaerobes**.
