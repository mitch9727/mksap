1. **Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)** is an **inflammatory condition**.
2. **Cigarette smoke** and other irritants activate both **macrophages** and **epithelial cells** within the respiratory tract.
3. Activated **epithelial cells** release several **neutrophil chemotactic factors** in COPD.
4. **Macrophages and recruited neutrophils** release **proteases** that cause **destruction of lung parenchyma** in COPD.
5. **Proteases** are normally inactivated by **antiproteases** such as **Î±1-antitrypsin**, but protease levels are increased in COPD.
6. **Oxidative stress** from irritants and inflammatory cells may lead to additional inflammation and tissue destruction in COPD.
7. **Fibrosis of the small airways** and **reduced elastic recoil of the lungs** lead to **static hyperinflation** in COPD.
8. **Mucin concentrations** are abnormally elevated in COPD.
9. Increased inflammation in COPD worsens **airflow obstruction** and decreases the ability to fully exhale.
10. In COPD, inhalation may be initiated before exhalation is completed, resulting in **dynamic hyperinflation (air trapping)**.
11. Radiographic signs of hyperinflation in COPD include **increased anteroposterior chest diameter** and **flattening of the diaphragm**.
12. **Diaphragmatic flattening** in COPD further limits the ability to increase breath volume during exertion.
13. Patients with COPD may use **accessory muscles** to increase tidal volume and may have an **increased respiratory rate** to augment minute ventilation.
14. These physiologic changes increase the **work of breathing** and contribute to the sensation of **dyspnea** in COPD.
