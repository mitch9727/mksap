### ü´Å Pulmonary Medicine: Tuberculous Pleural Effusion

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Tuberculous pleural effusion** is typically an **exudative effusion** with a predominance of **lymphocytes** on cell count.
2. **Pleural fluid adenosine deaminase (ADA)** is elevated in most tuberculous pleural effusions, with a **sensitivity of 90%**.
3. In **low-prevalence regions**, a **negative ADA test** has a **98.9% negative predictive value** for excluding **tuberculosis**.
4. In **high-prevalence regions**, a **pleural fluid ADA level >40 U/L** has a **positive predictive value of 98%** for **tuberculosis**.
5. False-positive pleural fluid ADA results can occur in **malignancy**, **bacterial empyema**, and **parapneumonic effusions**.
6. **Pleural biopsy** yields a **positive mycobacterial culture in >70%** of **tuberculous pleural effusions**, and histology may establish the diagnosis if **caseating granulomas** are seen.
7. **Pleural fluid acid-fast smear** and **culture** are often **negative** in **tuberculous pleural effusion**.
8. **Transthoracic echocardiography** is useful for evaluating pleural effusions caused by **heart failure**, which are usually **transudates**, but it is **not indicated** in an **exudative effusion** suspicious for **tuberculosis**.
9. **Pleural fluid triglyceride level >110 mg/dL (1.24 mmol/L)** suggests **chylothorax**, most often from **thoracic duct disruption** due to **surgery, trauma, or malignancy**.

#### üí¨ Extra
2. ADA is an enzyme present in lymphocytes, explaining its elevation in lymphocyte-predominant effusions such as tuberculosis.
6. Because of its invasiveness, pleural biopsy is considered a second-line diagnostic step after ADA testing.
9. Chylothorax is not a likely cause in this patient because the effusion is lymphocyte-predominant without trauma or surgery.

### üìò Related Text Derivations

#### ‚úÖ True Statements ‚Äî Tests for Tuberculous Effusion
1. **Tuberculosis** should be considered in a **lymphocyte-predominant exudative pleural effusion** of unclear cause.
2. **Pleural fluid acid-fast smear** has a **sensitivity <5%** for **tuberculous pleural effusion**.
3. **Pleural fluid mycobacterial culture** has a **sensitivity of ~30%** in **tuberculous pleural effusion** because of the **low mycobacterial load**.
4. **Pleural fluid adenosine deaminase (ADA)** is elevated in most **tuberculous pleural effusions**, with a **sensitivity of 90%**.
5. In **low-incidence countries**, a **negative ADA test** can help **exclude tuberculosis**.
6. **Pleural biopsy** is the most likely source to yield a **positive mycobacterial culture (sensitivity 50%)** and is useful for **histology**.
7. **Rapid nucleic acid amplification tests** on pleural fluid are increasingly used to detect **pleural tuberculosis**.

#### üí¨ Extra
2‚Äì3. The low sensitivity of smear and culture reflects the limited number of organisms present in pleural fluid.
6. Biopsy allows for both culture and histologic diagnosis, which is particularly useful when ADA results are inconclusive.

##### ‚úÖ True Statements (from Table: Sensitivity and Specificity of Criteria for Exudative Pleural Effusion)
1. **Combined Light&apos;s criteria** (‚â•1 of 3) for identifying **exudative pleural effusion** have **98% sensitivity** and **72% specificity**.
2. A **pleural fluid protein/serum protein ratio &gt;0.5** has **87% sensitivity** and **86% specificity** for **exudative pleural effusion**.
3. A **pleural fluid lactate dehydrogenase (LDH)/serum LDH ratio &gt;0.6** has **89% sensitivity** and **84% specificity** for **exudative pleural effusion**.
4. A **pleural fluid LDH level &gt;2/3 the upper limit of normal (ULN) for serum LDH** has **81% sensitivity** and **91% specificity** for **exudative pleural effusion**.
5. A **pleural fluid cholesterol level &gt;55 mg/dL (1.42 mmol/L)** has **67% sensitivity** and **94% specificity** for **exudative pleural effusion**.
6. The combined alternative criterion of **pleural fluid cholesterol &gt;55 mg/dL** or **pleural fluid LDH &gt;2/3 ULN for serum LDH** has **97% sensitivity** and **84% specificity** for **exudative pleural effusion**.

##### üí¨ Extra
1. Light‚Äôs criteria maximize **sensitivity** to avoid missing exudates; individual components and cholesterol-based alternatives vary in **specificity**.
4. Expansions: **LDH = lactate dehydrogenase**; **ULN = upper limit of normal**.
