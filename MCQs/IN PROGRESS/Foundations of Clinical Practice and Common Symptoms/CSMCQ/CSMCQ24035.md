### ü¶¥ Common Symptoms: Lateral Meniscus Tear Diagnosis

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Meniscal tears** usually result from an **acute twisting knee injury**, but they can also develop **insidiously** due to **chronic degeneration**.
2. **Meniscal tears** commonly cause **knee pain**, **locking**, **catching**, and **grinding** symptoms.
3. On physical examination for **meniscal tears**, **joint line tenderness** is often present on the **side of the injury**.
4. A **small or moderate knee effusion** may be present on examination in **meniscal tears**.
5. **Specialized tests of meniscal integrity**, including the **McMurray test**, **medial‚Äìlateral grind test**, and **Thessaly test**, are often **positive** in **meniscal tears**, but **no single test is highly sensitive or specific**.
6. A **combination of clinical findings** is **more diagnostically informative** for **meniscal tears** than **any single test**.
7. **Imaging studies** for **suspected meniscal tears** may **not be necessary** and are often **reserved for cases in which surgery is being considered**.
8. **Iliotibial band syndrome** is an **overuse condition** that causes **lateral knee pain** and **does not present suddenly**.
9. In **iliotibial band syndrome**, **tenderness** localizes to the **lateral femoral epicondyle** rather than the **joint line**, and **tests of meniscal integrity are negative**.
10. **Knee osteoarthritis** in **young adults without prior trauma** is **unlikely** and typically presents **insidiously**; **bony joint abnormalities** may be present on examination.
11. **Patellofemoral pain syndrome (PFPS)** is characterized by **poorly localized anterior knee pain** that **worsens with prolonged sitting**, **squatting**, **running**, and **using stairs**.
