1. **Anemia** in the setting of a **hip fracture** is common due to the fracture itself and may worsen postoperatively.
2. In the **perioperative setting**, patients should be **monitored for anemia**.
3. For **hospitalized patients** who are anemic and **hemodynamically stable**, the **Association for the Advancement of Blood and Biotherapies (AABB)** recommends a **restrictive transfusion strategy**.
4. A **hemoglobin threshold** of **less than 7 g/dL (70 g/L)** is recommended for most hospitalized patients under a restrictive transfusion strategy.
5. A **hemoglobin threshold of 7.5 g/dL (75 g/L)** may be considered for patients undergoing **cardiac surgery**.
6. A **hemoglobin threshold of 8 g/dL (80 g/L)** may be selected for patients undergoing **orthopedic surgery** or who have **underlying cardiovascular disease**.
7. A **more liberal transfusion strategy** has not been shown to improve outcomes such as **mortality**, **cardiovascular outcomes**, or **stroke**.
8. **Ongoing monitoring without transfusion** is appropriate for a hemodynamically stable patient with hemoglobin above the orthopedic surgery transfusion threshold.
9. **Colonoscopy** is not indicated for anemia due to **acute orthopedic injury and surgery**.
10. **Peripheral blood smear** is not indicated when anemia is attributable to **hip fracture and urgent orthopedic surgery**.
