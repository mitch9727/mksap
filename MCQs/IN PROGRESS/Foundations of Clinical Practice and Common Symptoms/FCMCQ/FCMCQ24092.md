1. **Physicians are legally and ethically obligated to disclose medical errors to patients.**
2. **When disclosing a medical error and its consequences, the physician should express concern and compassion for the patient.**
3. **Error disclosure should include an explanation of the course of events and how the error occurred.**
4. **Error disclosure should include the consequences of the event and a description of how the effects will be minimized or rectified.**
5. **Error disclosure should include steps that the system will take to reduce recurrences.**
6. **Information on how a medical error occurred may take time to gather, and patients should be informed of the process.**
7. **Assigning individual blame without further explanation or investigation is inappropriate in medical error disclosure.**
8. **Medical errors should be investigated using root cause analysis (RCA), process mapping, or other quality improvement (QI) tools that focus on system improvement rather than individual fault.**
9. **Adverse events should be reported to institutional tracking systems or the quality improvement department to enable prevention of similar incidents.**
10. **In addition to institutional reporting, the affected patient must be informed of the error.**
11. **Nondisclosure of a medical error may damage patient trust, anger the patient and family, and increase the likelihood of legal action.**
12. **Timely disclosure of a medical error accompanied by a genuine, compassionate apology is associated with greater patient forgiveness and a lower likelihood of legal action.**
