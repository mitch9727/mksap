### üß† Neurology: Venous Thromboembolism Prophylaxis After Intracerebral Hemorrhage

#### ‚úÖ True Statements
1. **Low-molecular-weight heparin (LMWH)** prophylaxis can be started **24 hours after intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH)** when **neuroimaging shows no active bleeding**, to prevent **deep venous thrombosis (DVT)**.
2. **Severe limb weakness (hemiparesis)** after **stroke** confers **high risk** for developing **DVT**.
3. **Deep venous thrombosis** is the **leading complication** of **hospitalization for stroke**.
4. **Deep venous thrombosis** is found in **approximately 11%** of **immobile patients** at **1 week** after **stroke**.
5. **Pulmonary embolism (PE)**, which typically originates from **DVT**, occurs in **approximately 1%** of **patients with stroke**.
6. **External sequential compression devices** may be used for **DVT prophylaxis** **before pharmacologic prophylaxis** is initiated after **ICH**.
7. **Aspirin** is **not effective** as **DVT prophylaxis** in **hospitalized patients** and **should not be used** for this purpose.
8. **Thigh-high graduated compression stockings** **do not prevent venous thromboembolism (VTE)** after **stroke with substantial leg weakness** and are associated with **increased skin complications** (breaks, ulcers, blisters, necrosis).
9. **Warfarin** **monotherapy** is **not adequate** for **immediate or short-term DVT prophylaxis** because its **anticoagulant effect is delayed** by **36 to 72 hours**.
10. **Early warfarin use** after **ICH** **increases the risk** for **expansion of intracranial hemorrhage** and is **not appropriate** for **prophylaxis** in this setting.

#### üí¨ Extra
1. The **24-hour LMWH start** assumes **repeat neuroimaging** (for example, **two CT scans 24 hours apart**) shows **no active bleeding** or **hemorrhage expansion**.
