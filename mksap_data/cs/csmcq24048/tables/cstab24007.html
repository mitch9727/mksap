High-Risk Clinical Characteristics in the Patient With Syncope a

Syncope in supine position

Syncope with exertion

New chest discomfort

Antecedent palpitations

History of structural heart abnormalities, ischemic heart disease, known arrhythmia, or heart failure

Pulmonary hypertension

Family history of sudden death

Sinus bradycardia (<40/min)

Systolic blood pressure <90 mm Hg

Hemoglobin <9 g/dL (90 g/L)

Abnormal ECG: new or previously unknown left bundle branch block, bifascicular block, Brugada pattern, acute ischemic changes, new non-sinus rhythm, prolonged QTc (>450 ms)
