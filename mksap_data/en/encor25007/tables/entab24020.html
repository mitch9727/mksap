Management of Hyperglycemic Crisis: Diabetic Ketoacidosis and Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar Syndrome

Fluids

Insulin (Regular)

Potassium

Correction of Acidosis

Assess volume status: for severe hypovolemia, give 0.9% saline or other crystalloid IV at 1 L/h initially; with mild hypovolemia, give 0.9% saline or other crystalloid at a rate to replace 50% of the estimated fluid deficit in the first 8-12 h.

When the plasma glucose level is less than 250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L), add 5% or 10% dextrose to the IV fluids.

In euglycemic DKA, begin 5% or 10% dextrose alongside 0.9% saline or other crystalloid at the start of insulin treatment.

For DKA a : give regular insulin, 0.1 U/kg/h, as an IV infusion b . When the plasma glucose level is <250 mg/dL (13.9 mmol/L), reduce dosage to 0.05 U/kg/h intravenously.

For HHS: give 0.05 U/kg/h of regular insulin intravenously.

For DKA, maintain the glucose level between 150 and 200 mg/dL (8.3-11.0 mmol/L) until acidosis is resolved. For HHS, target a glucose level between 200 and 250 mg/dL (11.0-13.9 mmol/L) until HHS is resolved.

After DKA/HHS resolves d and the patient is able to eat, start basal-bolus subcutaneous insulin regimen. Continue IV infusion for 1-2 h after subcutaneous insulin is given to ensure adequate insulin levels. In insulin-naive patients, give a total daily dose of 0.5-0.6 U/kg of insulin and adjust accordingly.

Establish adequate kidney function (urine output of about 0.5 mL/kg/h).

If serum potassium is <3.5 mEq/L (3.5 mmol/L), do not start insulin; instead, give IV potassium chloride, 10-20 mEq/L/h c until serum potassium level is >3.5 mEq/L (3.5 mmol/L). Then, start insulin and add 10-20 mEq of potassium chloride to each liter of IV fluids to keep the potassium level in the 4.0-5.0 mEq/L (4.0-5.0 mmol/L) range.

If the serum potassium level is >5.0 mEq/L (5.0 mmol/L), do not give potassium chloride; instead, start insulin and IV fluids and check the potassium level every 2 h.

If pH is <7.0, consider sodium bicarbonate, 100 mEq (100 mmol) in 400 mL of sterile water infused every 2 h to increase the pH to >7.0.

If pH is 7.0 or greater, do not give sodium bicarbonate.
