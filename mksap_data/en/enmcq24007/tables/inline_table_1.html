Screening Guidelines for Prediabetes or Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus in Asymptomatic Adults

ADA (2025)

USPSTF (2021)

Screening criteria

Screen adults with overweight (BMI ≥25, or ≥23 in persons of Asian ancestry) with at least one additional risk factor:

First-degree relative with diabetes

Race/ethnicity with high risk (e.g., African American, Latino, Native American, Asian American)

History of CVD

Physical inactivity

Hypertension (≥130/80 mm Hg or receiving antihypertensive therapy)

HDL cholesterol <35 mg/dL (0.90 mmol/L) and/or triglycerides >250 mg/dL (2.82 mmol/L)

Polycystic ovary syndrome

Other conditions associated with insulin resistance (severe obesity, acanthosis nigricans, metabolic dysfunction–associated steatotic liver disease)

All others: begin testing at age 35 years

Screen nonpregnant adults aged 35 to 70 years who have overweight or obesity

Screening for other modifiable risk factors for CVD

Overweight

Obesity

Physical inactivity

Hypertension

Abnormal lipid levels

Smoking

Additional screening considerations

Persons with HIV infection, exposure to high-risk medications, evidence of periodontal disease, or history of pancreatitis

Consider screening earlier if one of the following risk factors is present:

Family history of diabetes

History of gestational diabetes

Polycystic ovary syndrome

BMI ≥23 in Asian Americans

Race/ethnicity with high risk (Black, Hispanic/Latino, Asian American, American Indian/Alaskan Native, Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander)

Screening intervals

Rescreen at least every 3 years if results are normal

Monitor patients with a history of prediabetes annually

Test patients with a history of gestational diabetes every 1-3 years

Data supporting optimal screening intervals are limited. Rescreening every 3 years may be reasonable.
