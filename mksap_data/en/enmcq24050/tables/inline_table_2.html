Dynamic Testing for Pituitary Dysfunction

Indication

Test

Technique

Interpretation

ACTH (cortisol) deficiency

ACTH stimulation test

Measure baseline serum cortisol and ACTH levels.

Administer 250 μg of synthetic ACTH IM or IV.

Measure serum cortisol levels at 30 and 60 minutes.

A peak serum cortisol level >18 μg/dL (496.8 nmol/L) indicates a normal response; a lower threshold of 14 to 15 μg/dL (390-414 nmol/L) is sufficient when using newer, more specific immunoassays for measuring cortisol.

AVP disorder (DI)

Water deprivation test, followed by desmopressin challenge, if indicated a

Patient empties bladder, and baseline weight is measured. Start test at 8 am.

Measure urine volume and osmolality hourly.

Measure serum sodium, serum osmolality, and weight every 2 h.

The test is stopped after 8 h or when one of the following occurs:

Urine osmolality/serum osmolality is >2

Patient has lost >3% of body weight

Urine osmolality is stable for 2-3 h while serum osmolality rises

Serum osmolality >295 mOsm/kg H 2 O

Serum sodium >145 mEq/L (145 mmol/L)

Water deprivation test interpretation:

Urine osmolality >800 mOsm/kg H 2 O is a normal response to water deprivation, indicating that vasopressin production and peripheral effect are intact; diagnosis is most likely primary polydipsia.

Urine osmolality between 300 and 800 mOsm/kg H 2 O suggests partial AVP disorder.

Urine osmolality <300 mOsm/kg H 2 O is consistent with AVP disorder.

Desmopressin challenge to differentiate between AVP resistance (nephrogenic DI) and AVP deficiency (central DI):

With serum osmolality >295 mOsm/kg H 2 O and serum sodium >145 mEq/L (145 mmol/L), give desmopressin 2 μg subcutaneously.

Measure urine osmolality and urine volume hourly for 4 h after desmopressin.

Desmopressin challenge interpretation:

For patients with urine osmolality <300 mOsm/kg H 2 O before desmopressin challenge:

Increase in urine osmolality by >50% after desmopressin is consistent with complete AVP deficiency (central DI).

Increase in urine osmolality by <50% indicates complete AVP resistance (nephrogenic DI).

For those with urine osmolality 300-800 mOsm/kg H 2 O, consider partial AVP disorder.

GH excess (acromegaly)

Glucose tolerance test

75-g oral glucose tolerance test. Measure plasma glucose and GH at 0, 30, 60, 90, and 120 minutes.

GH <0.2 ng/mL (0.2 μg/L) is a normal response.

GH nadir ≥1.0 ng/mL (1.0 μg/L) (or ≥0.4 ng/mL [0.4 μg/L] on an ultrasensitive assay) is diagnostic of acromegaly.
