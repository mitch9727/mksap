Process of Motivational Interviewing

Engage

Obtain a genuine understanding of the patient's behavior in a nonjudgmental way.

Reflect back what the patient says, using slightly different wording if needed, to facilitate understanding and establish rapport.

Avoid making assumptions about the patient's motivation.

Focus

Listen for change talk from the patient (giving positive reasons for changing the behavior) versus sustain talk (eliciting why the patient might want to sustain the status quo behavior).

Acknowledge sustain talk and gently encourage the patient to elaborate during change talk :

Elaborate: “Tell me more.” “Yes, I see.”

Affirm: “You want to be a good role model for your children.”

Reflect: “It sounds like that really affected you.” “You're going to start running again.”

When reflecting, make a guess in the form of a statement.

Evoke

Help patient formulate and verbalize their new goals, what they are hoping for, reasons to change, and what would be a good first step.

If patient feels unable to express goals, hopes, and/or reasons to change, offer suggestions of what other patients have done in similar situations.

Plan

Discuss specific goals and resources to achieve goals and parameters for monitoring progress and evaluating success.

Identify barriers to success and make a plan to combat these barriers in advance.
