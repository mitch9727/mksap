Core Skills for Motivational Interviewing

Core Skill

Purpose

Example

O pen-ended questions

Elicit and explore the person's experiences, perspectives, and ideas.

“Your hemoglobin A 1c is high at 9.5%. Tell me what your thoughts are about that.”

A ffirmations

Emphasize strengths, efforts, and past successes to build the person's hope and confidence in their ability to change.

“You have been so successful at staying away from tobacco! How could we use those strengths to improve other aspects of your health?”

R eflections

Offer simple (repeating) and/or complex (deeper guesses) reflections, based on careful listening, to try to fully appreciate where the person is coming from.

“I'm hearing you say that work is so demanding that it is hard to fit exercise and meal planning into your busy day.”

S ummaries

Ensure mutual understanding and reinforce key points that the patient has expressed, including highlighting ambivalence and weighing pros and cons.

“So, on the one hand, you want to lose weight and don't want to be on more medications. On the other hand, you are having a hard time with meal planning and don't have time to exercise. Where do we go from here?”
