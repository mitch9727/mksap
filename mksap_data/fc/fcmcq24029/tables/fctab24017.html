SHARE Steps for Shared Decision Making

Step

Purpose

Sample statement

Step 1: S eek

Inform the patient that a choice exists and seek the patient's participation.

“Now that we have identified the problem, it's time for us to think about how we will manage it. I'd like for us to discuss all the options so we can find the option that you are most comfortable with.”

Step 2: H elp

Help the patient explore and compare treatment options, including risks and benefits.

“What have you heard about treatments for this condition?”

“Let's discuss the risks and benefits of each option.”

Step 3: A ssess

Assess the patient's values and preferences, and define what issues matter most to them.

“As you think about these options, what is most important to you?”

“Which side effects worry you the most?”

Step 4: R each

Reach a decision with the patient. Discern whether further information or tools (educational materials, decision aids) are needed.

“Before making a decision, do you have any additional questions?”

“Do you want to take more time or talk this over with your family members before making a decision?”

“Now that we've discussed options, which treatment do you think would be best for you?”

Step 5: E valuate

Evaluate the decision and its implementation, revisiting and modifying the treatment as necessary.

“Let's plan on reviewing how are you doing with this treatment in another 2 weeks.”
