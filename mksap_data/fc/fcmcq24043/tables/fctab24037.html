Selected Indications for Preoperative Laboratory Testing

Laboratory Test a

Preoperative Indications

Hemoglobin

History of anemia

Signs/symptoms or examination findings suggestive of anemia

Underlying disease that predisposes to anemia (e.g., kidney disease, myelodysplasia)

Expected substantial operative blood loss

Platelet count

History of thrombocytopenia or cirrhosis; signs or symptoms of bleeding or liver disease

Coagulation studies

Warfarin or heparin use

History of abnormal bleeding

Medical conditions that predispose to coagulopathy (e.g., liver disease, nutritional disorders, hemophilia)

Electrolytes

Diseases that predispose to electrolyte derangements (e.g., kidney disease)

Use of medications that may cause electrolyte abnormalities (e.g., diuretics, ACE inhibitors, ARBs)

Creatinine

Kidney disease

Expected large intraoperative fluid and blood pressure shifts

Creatinine is also used in preoperative cardiovascular risk calculators and for calculation of the MELD score

Liver chemistry tests (including bilirubin)

Cirrhosis, history of abnormal liver chemistry tests, or signs or symptoms of liver disease

Fasting glucose and hemoglobin A 1c

Known or suspected diabetes mellitus; measure hemoglobin A 1C if not obtained within the past 3 months

Urinalysis

Suspected urinary tract infection

Planned urologic procedures

Pregnancy test

Women of childbearing age

BNP, NT-proBNP, and cardiac troponin

May be helpful for further risk stratification in patients with poor or unknown functional capacity and known cardiovascular disease, age ≥65 years, or age ≥45 years with symptoms of cardiovascular disease
