Indications for Meningococcal Vaccination in Adults With Increased Risk for Meningococcal Disease

Population

MenACWY

MenB

Persistent complement component deficiencies (e.g., C5-C9, factor H, factor D, properdin) or persons taking complement inhibitors (e.g., eculizumab or ravulizumab)

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y

Primary vaccination followed by booster 1 y later and every 2-3 y thereafter

Functional or anatomic asplenia (including sickle cell disease)

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y

Primary vaccination followed by booster 1 y later and every 2-3 y thereafter

HIV infection

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y

Same as general population

Microbiologists routinely exposed to Neisseria meningitidis

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y

Primary vaccination followed by booster 1 y later and every 2-3 y thereafter

Exposure to vaccine-preventable serogroup meningococcal disease outbreak

Single dose if ≥5 y since vaccination

Primary vaccination; booster if ≥1 y since primary series

Travel or residence in country with hyperendemic or epidemic meningococcal disease

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y

Same as general population

First-year college students living in residence halls

Primary vaccination

Same as general population

Military recruits

Primary vaccination followed by booster every 5 y (depending on military assignment)

Same as general population
