Acute mesenteric ischemia

Severe periumbilical pain; pain is out of proportion to examination

Mesenteric CT angiography

Urgent laparotomy if peritoneal signs; embolectomy or thrombectomy

Chronic mesenteric ischemia

Mesenteric atherosclerosis

Postprandial pain, sitophobia, weight loss

Mesenteric CT or MR angiography

Angioplasty with stenting or surgical bypass

Low-flow states (hypovolemia or hypotension)
