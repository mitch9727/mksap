First-Line and Salvage Treatment for Helicobacter pylori Infection

Treatment Regimena

Comments/Indications

First-Line Treatment

Optimized bismuth quadruple therapy

PPI, bismuth subcitrate or subsalicylate, tetracycline, metronidazole

May also be used for patients with a penicillin allergy

Rifabutin triple therapy

Omeprazole, amoxicillin, rifabutin

Alternative to bismuth quadruple therapy

PCAB dual therapy

Vonoprazan, amoxicillin

Alternative to bismuth quadruple therapy

PCAB-clarithromycin triple therapy

Vonoprazan, clarithromycin, amoxicillin

Avoid in patients with a history of macrolide exposure

Should generally be reserved for patients with documented clarithromycin sensitivity

Salvage Treatment

Optimized bismuth quadruple therapy

PPI, bismuth subcitrate or subsalicylate, tetracycline, metronidazole

If previous therapy was not bismuth quadruple therapy

Failure of clarithromycin-based therapy

Rifabutin triple therapy

PPI, amoxicillin, rifabutin

Failure of bismuth-based quadruple therapy

Levofloxacin triple therapy

PPI, levofloxacin, amoxicillin

Proven levofloxacin-sensitive Helicobacter pylori

Failure of optimized bismuth quadruple therapy or rifabutin triple therapy

Nonavailability of bismuth quadruple therapy or rifabutin triple therapy

PCAB-clarithromycin triple therapy

Vonoprazan, clarithromycin, amoxicillin

Proven clarithromycin-sensitive H. pylori

Failure of optimized bismuth quadruple therapy or rifabutin triple therapy

Nonavailability of bismuth quadruple therapy or rifabutin triple therapy
