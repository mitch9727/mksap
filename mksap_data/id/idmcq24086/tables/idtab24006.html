Treatment of Skin Infections

Infection

Treatment

Erysipelas or cellulitis a

Mild: PO penicillin, cephalexin, dicloxacillin, clindamycin

Moderate: IV penicillin, ceftriaxone, cefazolin, clindamycin b

Severe: Surgical assessment for possible necrotizing infection and empiric IV vancomycin plus piperacillin-tazobactam, imipenem, or meropenem

Necrotizing fasciitis

Polymicrobial infection: Surgical assessment/debridement and combination therapy such as vancomycin plus piperacillin-tazobactam or imipenem or meropenem

Streptococcus pyogenes or Clostridium species: Surgical assessment/debridement and penicillin plus clindamycin

Aeromonas hydrophila : Surgical assessment/debridement and ciprofloxacin or ceftriaxone plus doxycycline

Vibrio vulnificus : Surgical assessment/debridement and third-generation cephalosporin (ceftazidime, ceftriaxone, or cefotaxime) plus doxycycline

Folliculitis

Staphylococcus aureus : Usually self-limited; can use topical mupirocin, clindamycin, or retapamulin; systemic antibiotics usually not needed

Pseudomonas aeruginosa (i.e., hot tub folliculitis): Usually self-limited; PO ciprofloxacin usually reserved for immunosuppressed host or for persistent or severe infection

Furuncle, carbuncle, or abscess

Mild: Incision and drainage; consider empiric PO anti-MRSA antibiotic such as trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole or doxycycline c

Moderate: Incision and drainage plus empiric PO trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole or PO doxycycline pending culture and susceptibilities c

Severe: Incision and drainage plus empiric vancomycin, daptomycin, linezolid, telavancin, or ceftaroline pending culture and susceptibilities
