Diagnostic Criteria for Toxic Shock Syndrome

Staphylococcal TSS a

Streptococcal TSS b

Fever >38.9 °C (102.0 °F)

Definite case

Isolation of GABHS from a sterile site (blood, cerebrospinal fluid, operative wound)

Systolic blood pressure ≤90 mm Hg

Probable case

Isolation of GABHS from a nonsterile site (throat, vagina, skin lesion)

Diffuse macular rash with subsequent desquamation, especially on palms and soles

Hypotension (systolic pressure ≤90 mm Hg)

Involvement of ≥3 of the following organ systems:

Gastrointestinal (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)

Muscular (severe myalgia or fivefold or greater increase in serum creatine kinase level)

Mucous membrane (hyperemia of vagina, conjunctivae, or pharynx)

Kidney (blood urea nitrogen or serum creatinine level at least twice the upper limit of normal)

Liver (bilirubin, aspartate aminotransferase, or alanine aminotransferase concentration twice the upper limit of normal)

Blood (platelet count <100,000/µL [100 × 10 9 /L])

Central nervous system (disorientation without focal neurologic signs)

Presence of ≥2 of the following findings:

Kidney (acute kidney injury or failure)

Liver (elevated aminotransferase concentrations)

Skin (erythematous macular rash, soft tissue necrosis)

Blood (coagulopathy, including thrombocytopenia and disseminated intravascular coagulation)

Pulmonary (acute respiratory distress syndrome)

Negative results on serologic testing for Rocky Mountain spotted fever, leptospirosis, and measles; negative blood or cerebrospinal fluid cultures for organisms other than Staphylococcus aureus
