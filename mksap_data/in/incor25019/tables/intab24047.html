Pharmacotherapy for Generalized Anxiety Disorder

Class of Agent

Specific Agent, Therapeutic Dose

Adverse Effects and Notes

First-line medications: SSRIs and SNRIs

As a class: nausea, diarrhea, decreased appetite, restlessness, insomnia, somnolence, impaired sexual function, and hyponatremia

SSRI

Escitalopram, 10-20 mg/d

QT prolongation

10 mg/d is the maximum dose recommended for older adults and those with hepatic impairment

SSRI

Paroxetine, 20-60 mg/d

More sexual dysfunction, weight gain, and sedation; discontinuation syndrome not uncommon

Increased drug interactions, including strong CYP2D6 inhibition

SSRI

Sertraline, 50-200 mg/d

Higher incidence of gastrointestinal distress

SNRI

Duloxetine, 60-120 mg/d

Gastrointestinal distress (less than with venlafaxine)

SNRI

Venlafaxine extended-release, 75-225 mg/d

Higher incidence of gastrointestinal distress; increased blood pressure

Second-line medications

Azapirones

Buspirone, 15-30 mg/d

Ineffective for comorbid disorders; dizziness, drowsiness

Benzodiazepines

Alprazolam, 0.25-1 mg/d; lorazepam, 0.5-2 mg/d

As a class: falls, memory impairment, risk for dependence

Anticonvulsant

Pregabalin, 150-600 mg/d

Sedation, dizziness, peripheral edema

Used as an adjuvant to first-line medications

Use with caution in patients with renal impairment

Antihistamine

Hydroxyzine, 50-100 mg four times daily

Sedation, dry mouth, confusion, and urine retention
