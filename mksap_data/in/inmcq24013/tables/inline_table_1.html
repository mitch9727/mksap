Clinical Presentation, Evaluation, and Management of Vaginitis

Cause of Vaginitis

Clinical Presentation

Evaluation

Management

Symptomatic bacterial vaginosis (pregnant or not pregnant)

Malodorous or “fishy” vaginal discharge

Increased thin white or gray discharge

Symptoms other than malodor may be minimal

Diagnosis requires three of four findings (Amsel criteria):

1. pH >4.5

2. Thin homogenous discharge

3. KOH amine whiff test result positive

4. Saline wet mount with >20% epithelial clue cells (see Figure: Clue Cells on Wet Mount)

Positive NAAT result

Metronidazole: 500 mg orally twice daily for 7 da or vaginal gel (0.75%) 5 g intravaginally at bedtime for 5 nights

Clindamycin: Vaginal cream (2%) 5 g intravaginally for 7 nights

Sexual partners do not require treatment

Avoid sexual activity (or use condoms) during treatment

Vulvovaginal candidiasis

Itching, irritation, dysuria, dyspareunia, vulvodynia, excoriation, erythema, fissures

Increased thick white discharge

pH ≤4.5

Negative KOH amine whiff test result

KOH wet mount shows hyphae, pseudohyphae, or yeast (see Figure: Vulvovaginal Candidiasis Findings on Wet Mount)

Positive NAAT result

Sexual partners do not require treatment

Uncomplicatedb

Over-the-counter intravaginal agents:

Clotrimazole: 1% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 7-14 d; or 2% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 3 d

Miconazole: 2% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 7 d; or 4% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 3 d; or 100-mg vaginal suppository, one suppository daily for 7 d; or 200-mg vaginal suppository, one suppository for 3 d; or 1200-mg vaginal suppository, one suppository for 1 d

Tioconazole: 6.5% ointment 5 g intravaginally in a single application

Prescription intravaginal agents:

Butoconazole: 2% cream (single-dose bioadhesive product) 5 g intravaginally in a single application

Terconazole: 0.4% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 7 d; or 0.8% cream 5 g intravaginally daily for 3 d; or 80-mg vaginal suppository, one suppository daily for 3 d

Prescription oral agent:

Fluconazole: 150 mg orally as a single dose

Complicatedc

Longer duration of initial oral or topical treatment, followed by maintenance therapy as needed:

Recurrent:

Fluconazole: 150 mg orally every 3 d for a total of three doses; or topical imidazole therapy for 7-14 nights

Maintenance therapy for refractory or recurrent symptoms: Fluconazole, 150 mg orally weekly for 6 mo

Severe:

Topical azole for 7-14 d or fluconazole, 150 mg for two doses separated by 72 h

Nonalbicans species:

Nonfluconazole topical azole for 7-14 d

Pregnancy:

Topical azole for 7 d; do not use fluconazole

Trichomoniasis

Increased discolored discharge (yellowish, green, gray, and/or frothy)

Dyspareunia, dysuria, itch, erythema, postcoital bleeding

Punctate cervical hemorrhages (“strawberry” cervix)

Negative KOH amine whiff test result

Saline wet mount with trichomonads, leukocytes (see Figure: Trichomonads on Wet Mount)

Positive NAAT or rapid assay result

For women: oral metronidazole, 500 mg twice daily for 7 d, or oral tinidazole, 2 g as a single dose.

For men: oral metronidazole, 2 g as a single dose, or oral tinidazole, 2 g as a single dose

Treatment of sexual partners is recommended to prevent reinfection
