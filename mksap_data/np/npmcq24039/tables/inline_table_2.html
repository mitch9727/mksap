Kidney Stone Risk Factors Based on Stone Type and Therapy

Stone Type

Risk Factors

Therapy

Calcium oxalate

Clinical: hyperparathyroidism; fat malabsorption; excessive vitamin D or C intake

Biochemical: hypercalciuria; hyperoxaluria; hypocitraturia

Increase fluids

Decrease sodium intake

Decrease animal protein intake

Maintain adequate dietary calcium

Low oxalate diet

Potassium citrate or bicarbonate

Consider thiazide diuretics

Calcium phosphate

Clinical: distal renal tubular acidosis; hyperparathyroidism; carbonic anhydrase inhibitors

Biochemical: elevated urine pH (>6.0-6.5); hypercalciuria; hypocitraturia

Increase fluids

Decrease sodium intake

Decrease animal protein intake

Maintain adequate dietary calcium

Treat hyperparathyroidism

Potassium citrate or bicarbonate

Consider thiazide diuretics

Uric acid

Clinical: metabolic syndrome; gout; diarrheal illnesses; metabolic acidosis

Biochemical: low urine pH (<5.5); hyperuricosuria

Increase fluids

Potassium citrate or bicarbonate

Acetazolamide

Allopurinol

Struvite

Clinical: chronic urinary tract infections with urea-splitting organisms

Biochemical: elevated urine pH (>7.5)

Treat infection

Urologic intervention

Cystine

Clinical: genetic condition, thus may have strong family history; young age at onset

Biochemical: elevated urine cystine; low urine pH

Increase fluids

Potassium citrate or bicarbonate

Acetazolamide

Penicillamine

Tiopronin
