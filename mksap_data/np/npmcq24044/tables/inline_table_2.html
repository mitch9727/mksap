Hypertension in Pregnancy

Category

Diagnostic Criteria

Chronic HTN

SBP ≥140 mm Hg or DBP ≥90 mm Hg

Starting before pregnancy or before 20 weeks of gestation, or persists longer than 12 weeks postpartum

Gestational HTN

SBP ≥140 mm Hg or DBP ≥90 mm Hg

Starting after 20 weeks of gestation without end-organ damage and resolves within 12 weeks of delivery

Preeclampsia

See Table: Diagnostic Criteria for Preeclampsia

Eclampsia

Preeclampsia plus seizures

Acute-onset severe HTN

SBP ≥160 mm Hg and/or DBP ≥110 mm Hg

If persists >15 minutes, requires emergent therapy: IV labetalol, IV hydralazine, or immediate-release oral nifedipine
