Nonpharmacologic Causes of Acute Kidney Injury

Cause

Examples

Prerenal

Volume depletion

Renal losses; GI fluid losses; hemorrhage; burns

Decreased cardiac output

Heart failure; massive pulmonary embolus; acute coronary syndrome

Systemic vasodilation

Sepsis; liver cirrhosis; anaphylaxis; anesthesia

Renal vasoconstriction

Early sepsis; hepatorenal syndrome; acute hypercalcemia

Increased intra-abdominal pressure and/or reduced venous flow

Abdominal compartment syndrome; venous outflow obstruction

Intrinsic

Acute tubular necrosis

Ischemic: prolonged prerenal AKI from hypovolemia, hypotension, or other causes of hypoperfusion

Sepsis-related AKI

Pigment: rhabdomyolysis; intravascular hemolysis

Acute interstitial nephritis

Causes of acute interstitial nephritis are similar to those for chronic tubulointerstitial nephritis. Acute interstitial nephritis may lead to chronic tubulointerstitial nephritis with protracted exposurea.
