Common Differential Diagnoses

Initial Diagnostic Tests

Treatment

Infectious

E.g., genitourinary, respiratory, gastrointestinal, skin

Specific central nervous system infections (e.g., prion disease, progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy)

CBC, blood culture, urinalysis and culture where indicated, chest radiography

Other studies as indicated by clinical findings (e.g., wound culture, lumbar puncture)

Antimicrobial therapy based on specific infection

Metabolic

Electrolyte disturbances, disorders of glucose metabolism, uremia, liver failure, nutritional deficiencies, dehydration

BMP, phosphorus, calcium, liver chemistry tests, TSH, vitamin B 12

If clinically indicated: specific serum/urine tests

Correct electrolyte and/or volume abnormality as clinically indicated

If clinically suggestive of Wernicke encephalopathy, treat empirically with IV thiamine before replenishing glucose

Toxic

Prescription and nonprescription drugs and nonprescribed substances; general anesthesia; alcohol or drug withdrawal; heavy metals

Medication review, urine drug screen

If clinically indicated: heavy metal panel, specific serum/urine tests

Stop use of offending agent; other specific therapy (e.g., chelation therapy) if indicated

Seizure-related

Convulsive and nonconvulsive seizures, postictal state, alcohol or benzodiazepine withdrawal seizures

Neuroimaging, routine or continuous EEG after acute management

See Seizures and Epilepsy b

Traumatic

Traumatic brain injury, subdural hematoma

If clinically indicated: neuroimaging

Neurosurgery/acute trauma surgery consultation if indicated

Normal-pressure hydrocephalus

For normal-pressure hydrocephalus only: large-volume lumbar puncture (>30 mL) that is preceded and followed (30 min after) by a timed 10-meter gait examination

For normal-pressure hydrocephalus: if gait examination improved after lumbar puncture, refer to neurosurgery for evaluation for ventriculoperitoneal shunt placement

Inflammatory

Autoimmune diseases, including paraneoplastic neurologic syndromes; demyelination

If indicated: neuroimaging, c-ANCA, p-ANCA, ANA, ESR/CRP, evaluation for Sjögren disease and sarcoidosis, rheumatoid factors, lumbar puncture, autoimmune encephalitis panel and/or paraneoplastic panel

Directed therapy based on cause, often with immunosuppressive therapy

Oncologic

Lymphoma, glioblastoma, metastasis, leukemia

Brain MRI with contrast, lumbar puncture (CSF cytology/cytometry)

See Neuro-oncology c

Other

Uncontrolled pain (including migraine, back pain, postoperative pain), fecal impaction and urinary retention, environment (post–acute care setting, prolonged hospitalization and ICU stay), primary psychiatric disorder

Diagnosis is made clinically when evaluation is unremarkable
