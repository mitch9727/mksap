Multiple Sclerosis Diagnostic Criteria

In the presence of a suggestive clinical presentation with exclusion of other better explanations, MS may be diagnosed with:

Lesions in ≥2 CNS sites a

AND at least one of the following:

CSF positivity b

≥6 lesions with CVS c

Dissemination in time d

Lesions present in four or five CNS locations a

≥2 spinal cord lesions and >12 months of progressive symptoms

As above

Lesions in ≥1 CNS site a (regardless of progression)

AND at least one of the following combinations:

CSF positivity b AND ≥6 lesions with CVS c

CSF positivity b AND ≥1 PRL e

Dissemination in time d AND ≥6 lesions with CVS c

Dissemination in time d AND ≥1 PRL e
