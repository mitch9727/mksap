Contraindications to Intravenous Thrombolysis in Adults With Acute Ischemic Stroke

Absolute Exclusion Criteria

Risk Versus Benefit Requires Consideration

Significant head trauma or previous stroke in the past 3 months

Suspicion of subarachnoid hemorrhage or CT with hemorrhage

Recent intracranial or spinal surgery

History of intracranial hemorrhage

Blood pressure ≥185/110 mm Hg despite treatment

Structural GI malignancy

Active bleeding diathesis

Platelet count <100,000/μL (100 × 10 9 /L)

Heparin within 48 hours with an activated partial thromboplastin time above normal range

Current use of anticoagulant with INR >1.7

Current use of non–vitamin K antagonist anticoagulants (within 48 hours) with associated elevated relevant laboratory tests

Current use of direct thrombin inhibitors or direct factor Xa inhibitors (unless laboratory tests are normal or no dose for 48 hours)

Noncontrast head CT–demonstrated multilobar infarction with >1/3 of the hemisphere involved

Rapidly improving nondisabling symptoms a

Pregnancy

Seizure at onset

Major surgery or serious trauma within 14 days

Recent GI or genitourinary bleeding within 21 days

Recent acute myocardial infarction

Blood glucose <50 mg/dL (2.8 mmol/L) or >400 mg/dL (22.2 mmol/L) that is normalized with persistent deficits

Known intracranial neoplasm, intracranial aneurysm, or arteriovenous malformation

Preexisting dementia or severe disability

Noncompressible site arterial puncture within 7 days

Lumbar puncture within 7 days

Recent major extracranial trauma

STEMI within the last 3 months
