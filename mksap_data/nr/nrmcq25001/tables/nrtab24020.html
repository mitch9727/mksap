Examples of Focal Preserved Consciousness Seizures a

Lobe

Clinical Presentation

Frontal

Focal contralateral motor activity (clonic jerking), hypermotor activity

Temporal

Epigastric rising sensation (nausea, butterflies); aphasia; déjà vu; fear; elation; auditory, olfactory, and gustatory sensations; autonomic symptoms

Parietal

Focal contralateral numbness or paresthesias

Occipital

Contralateral homonymous visual loss or simple hallucinations (dots, lines, flashes of light)
