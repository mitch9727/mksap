Recommendations for Breast Cancer Screening in Women at Average Risk

Expert Group

Recommendation

American Cancer Society (2015)

Age 40-44 y: Provide women with the opportunity to begin annual screening mammography

Age 45-54 y: Perform annual screening mammography

Age ≥55 y: Perform biennial screening mammography with the opportunity to continue annual screening

Do not perform CBE for breast cancer screening

American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (2017)

Age 25-39 y: May offer CBE every 1-3 y

Age 40-75 y: Perform annual or biennial screening mammography based on a shared decision-making process; offer annual CBE based on a shared decision-making process

Age >75 y: Engage women in a shared decision-making process about discontinuing screening

American College of Physicians (2019)

Age <40 y: Do not perform screening

Age 40-49 y: Potential harms of screening mammography outweigh benefits in average-risk women. Discuss benefits, harms, and patient preferences; order biennial mammography screening only if an informed woman requests it

Age 50-74 y: Encourage biennial mammography screening

Age ≥75 y or life expectancy <10 y: Do not perform screening

Do not use CBE to screen for breast cancer in average-risk women of all ages

American College of Radiology (2017)

Age 40 y: Begin annual screening mammography

National Comprehensive Cancer Network (2022)

Age 25-39 y: Clinical encounter (medical and family history, risk assessment/risk reduction counseling, and preferably CBE) every 1-3 y

Age 40 y: Begin annual screening mammography, preferably with tomosynthesis, and annual clinical encounter

Breast awareness, defined as having familiarity with one's breasts and reporting changes to the health care provider, is recommended for all age-groups

U.S. Preventive Services Task Force (2024)

Age 40-74 y: Perform biennial screening mammography (grade B)

Age ≥75 y: Insufficient evidence to assess the balance of benefits and harms of screening mammography (I statement)
